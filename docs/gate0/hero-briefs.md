# Founding Hero Briefs

**Visual direction:** Storybook Wildguard  
**Owner contribution:** Hero trio and personalities locked 2026-07-10

These briefs translate the owner's characters into gameplay, animation, writing,
and attachment rules. They preserve the owner's ideas rather than replacing them
with additional lore.

## Greg the Fox — Pouncer

### Character

Greg has the manner of a very proper older British gentleman. He is composed,
precise, unfailingly courteous, and mildly disappointed by unnecessary fuss.

The joke is not that Greg is weak or out of touch. He remains calm and impeccably
polite while becoming an increasingly impossible woodland chimera.

### Gameplay identity

- Fast, deliberate movement and forward pressure.
- Rewards close escapes without requiring an active button.
- **Automatic instinct — Rush Rake:** movement charges a three-wave claw burst at
  the nearest cluster in Greg's movement-facing hemisphere. Near-misses shorten
  the remaining charge distance.
- Natural affinity: precision, counterattack, quills, sonar, and orderly volleys.

### Animation and presentation

- Upright head carriage and economical idle movement.
- Tiny disapproving ear flick when enemies approach from behind.
- Attacks feel measured rather than frantic.
- Mythic mutations remain visually balanced and groomed during early/mid stages;
  late stages may become strange, but their arrangement should still feel
  intentional.

### Writing voice

- Short, formal, dry lines.
- Avoid dense British slang or parody accent spelling.
- Example tone: “Right. Let us deal with this properly.”

## Benny the Bull — Bastion

### Character

Benny is very nervous and worries that everyone sees him as too big, clumsy, or
dangerous. He tries to take up less space than he physically can.

His arc is about discovering that being large and protective is valuable. The
game should be affectionate toward his anxiety, never make his size the target of
cruel humor, and never require him to become aggressive to become confident.

### Gameplay identity

- Slow, durable, high knockback resistance.
- Converts defensive reactions into automatic area damage.
- **Automatic instinct — Brace Bloom:** Benny periodically gains a one-hit guard.
  When struck—or when the guard expires—it releases a shockwave that defensive
  attachments can modify.
- Natural affinity: shells, stomps, knockback, companion protection, and stored
  force.

### Animation and presentation

- Careful steps, tucked posture, and occasional worried glance at nearby props.
- Hit reaction begins as a startled flinch and resolves into a protective brace.
- Early attachments stay close to the body because Benny dislikes taking up more
  room; Mythic forms finally open into broad fortress silhouettes.
- Victory posture should be relieved and proud rather than dominant.

### Writing voice

- Gentle, apologetic, and concerned about collateral damage.
- Avoid constant stammering; anxiety is not his only trait.
- Example tone: “Oh! Sorry—was that shockwave me?”

## Gracie the Alpaca — Surveyor

### Character

Gracie is a twee hipster who loves matcha and anything on trend—but not *too*
trendy. She has strong taste, enjoys discovering things before everyone else, and
will abandon a fashion the moment it becomes obvious.

Her humor comes from treating strange biological mutations like carefully curated
finds. She should be perceptive and capable, not merely shallow.

### Gameplay identity

- Medium-speed precision, marking, orbitals, and support/control.
- **Automatic instinct — Keen Orbit:** a small neutral scout circles Gracie and
  marks the highest-health visible enemy. The first hit on a fresh mark critically
  strikes and splashes a weaker mark to nearby fodder.
- Natural affinity: companions, sonar, weak points, charm, and unusual recipe
  discovery.

### Animation and presentation

- Curious head tilts and confident, dainty foot placement.
- Evaluates new attachments with a brief approving or skeptical glance.
- Early/mid attachments use curated complementary colors; late mutations become
  intentionally asymmetrical while preserving a styled overall palette.
- Companion orbits should feel arranged, not chaotic.

### Writing voice

- Warm, observant, lightly precious, and quick to distinguish “current” from
  “already everywhere.”
- Do not overload every line with contemporary slang that will age quickly.
- Example tone: “Electric tails are having a moment. Quietly, of course.”

## Trio dynamics

- Greg supplies composure and dry practicality.
- Benny supplies empathy and defensive concern.
- Gracie supplies curiosity and aesthetic confidence.
- Greg treats Gracie's trend forecasts as weather reports.
- Gracie sincerely appreciates Benny's protective silhouette before he does.
- Benny is least nervous around Greg because Greg never comments on his size.

Keep story delivery lightweight: short selection lines, unlock text, victory
reactions, and Field Guide notes. Do not build a dialogue system or voiced
cutscenes for the hobby prototype.

## Attachment direction by hero

Attachments use the same mechanical definitions but may receive hero-specific
placement, scale, and palette profiles:

| Hero | Placement bias | Palette treatment | Mutation arc |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Greg | symmetrical and compact | forest teal, brass gold, cream | proper naturalist → impossible but immaculate chimera |
| Benny | close-fitting early, broad late | moss green, warm ochre, soft blue | apologetic gentle giant → protective living fortress |
| Gracie | curated asymmetry and companions | matcha green, lilac, mango accent | tasteful early adopter → beautifully strange tastemaker |

No attachment receives different combat math solely because of the hero's
personality. Hero differences come from instincts, base stats, affinity weights,
and visual placement profiles.

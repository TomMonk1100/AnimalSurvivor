# Gate 0 Market and Comprehension Test

Status: in progress.

This test is qualitative concept validation, not a sales forecast.

## Pitch A — Body-as-loadout

**Headline:** Build the animal. Become the legend.

**Subhead:** Survive the swarm while every power visibly changes your hero. Grow
quills, sonar ears, beetle armor, electric tails, and stranger combinations until
your cute animal becomes a one-of-a-kind mythic creature.

**Proof bullets:**

- Movement-only combat; your build fights automatically.
- Every major upgrade attaches to the animal you can see.
- Pair animal traits into dramatic mythic evolutions.
- Save each final creature to your Field Guide.

## Pitch B — Evolution fantasy

**Headline:** Every run creates a new species of hero.

**Subhead:** Start small, outgrow the swarm, and evolve a familiar animal into a
beautifully bizarre champion. Your final silhouette is the story of every choice
you made.

**Proof bullets:**

- Deep recipes underneath one-stick play.
- Cute beginnings and increasingly strange transformations.
- Read the build directly from horns, wings, shells, tails, and companions.
- Discover and name favorite forms.

## Core test comparison

Show each tester two otherwise equivalent level-up cards:

- **Invisible card:** “Hardened Hide — +20% armor.”
- **Visible card:** “Beetle Carapace — +20% armor; a shell appears on the hero and
  stores blocked force for later evolution.”

Do not explain the project hypothesis before the tester chooses.

For the strongest test, create two otherwise identical 15-second mockups:

- **V condition:** six upgrades visibly add or evolve body parts.
- **S condition:** the same attacks, timing, damage, upgrade names, sound, camera,
  and enemy count, but the animal model never changes.

Counterbalance order: testers 1–5 see V then S; testers 6–10 see S then V. The
visual card comparison remains the fallback if the two mockups cannot be produced
without changing other variables.

## Ten-person interview script

1. Show one board for five seconds. “What kind of game do you think this is?”
2. “What do you think changes during a run?”
3. Ask the tester to choose between the invisible and visible card above. “Why?”
4. Show the final creature. “Name three powers you think it has.”
5. “Can you still tell what animal it started as? What cues tell you?”
6. “Which stage looks most exciting to play, and which looks least readable?”
7. Read Pitch A, then Pitch B in randomized order. “Which would make you click
   Play? Repeat the better pitch in your own words.”
8. “Would you start another run to discover a different final form?”
9. “What looks generic, confusing, or too much like another game?”
10. “What is the one transformation you would want to see?”

## Decision thresholds

- Pass: at least 6/10 choose the visible adaptation and at least 7/10 accurately
  repeat that visible body changes are the build.
- Revise: art is liked but fewer than 7/10 understand the mechanic, or final-form
  readability is below 7/10.
- Stop/pivot: fewer than 6/10 prefer visible adaptations after one targeted
  revision, or the transformation is consistently described as cosmetic only.

## Competitor teardowns

Figures are snapshots from 2026-07-09 and will change.

### 1. Everything is Crab — closest threat and strongest validation

- **Hook:** 125+ animal-inspired evolutions visibly assemble a strange creature.
- **Signal:** [the publisher reported 500,000 sales](https://wearesecretmode.com/news/everything-is-crab-patch-1-point-1)
  after the May 2026 launch; the [Steam page](https://store.steampowered.com/app/3526710/Everything_is_Crab/)
  shows thousands of purchaser reviews.
- **What worked:** transformation is the headline and the creature is inherently
  screenshot-worthy.
- **Response:** differentiate with low-poly 3D, movement-only bullet-heaven flow,
  named heroic animals, body-slot readability, and paired combat evolutions. Do
  not pitch merely “animals that mutate.”

### 2. FuwaFuwa Survivors — low-traction near-match

- **Hook:** cute 3D animals, taming, 20+ skills, 70+ enchantments, and “1,000+
  combinations.”
- **Signal:** its [Steam page](https://store.steampowered.com/app/4043830/FuwaFuwa_Survivors/)
  showed only eight purchaser reviews at the research snapshot.
- **Lesson:** animals, 3D presentation, and combination counts do not create
  discovery by themselves.
- **Response:** the altered hero must dominate every trailer frame and upgrade
  decision. Content count is supporting proof, never the hook.

### 3. Temtem: Swarm — production-value and IP benchmark

- **Hook:** established collectible creatures, fixed evolutions, large bosses,
  and online co-op.
- **Signal:** [Steam](https://store.steampowered.com/app/2510960/Temtem_Swarm/)
  shows substantial review traction and a mostly/very positive reception depending
  on language and snapshot.
- **Opportunity:** its creature form changes are predefined; they do not visually
  record each run's chosen body build.
- **Response:** prove that two players using the same hero end with clearly
  different animals. Do not chase co-op or roster size.

### 4. Supercat Survivors — theme plus combinations remains modest

- **Hook:** cats, weapons/items, multiple modes, and large combination claims.
- **Signal:** [Steam](https://store.steampowered.com/app/3415300/Supercat_Survivors_Meow_or_Die/)
  shows a small but generally positive review base.
- **Lesson:** players who find the game may like it, but cat theming and build
  quantity do not guarantee reach.
- **Response:** attachments must communicate distinct behaviors through the
  silhouette; final portraits should be legible without stats.

### 5. Animal Survivors — generic-clone floor

- **Hook:** free, cute, simplified 3D survivor-like.
- **Signal:** [Steam](https://store.steampowered.com/app/2224660/Animal_Survivors/)
  and [SteamDB](https://steamdb.info/app/2224660/charts/) show very low durable
  traction.
- **Lesson:** removing price friction does not rescue an undifferentiated animal
  reskin.
- **Response:** reject any prototype whose footage can be summarized only as
  “Vampire Survivors, but the character is an animal.”

## Stronger quantitative scorecard

In addition to the simple results table above, record:

- V/S viewing order and A/B pitch order;
- visible and invisible upgrades recalled after a 30-second distraction;
- upgrade excitement for each condition on a 1–7 scale;
- expected run variety on a 1–7 scale;
- preferred version for three runs;
- which version feels more consequential and shareable;
- whether the tester independently says that the animal embodies the build;
- clutter/readability objections and strongest verbatim reason for/against.

The stronger proceed threshold is 7/10 preferring V, mean V excitement at least
1.0 point above S, 7/10 calling V more shareable, at least 50% more upgrade recall
for V, and no more than 3/10 raising serious final-form readability concerns.
